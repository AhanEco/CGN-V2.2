The Three Theoretical Pillars

1. Monetary Triadic Closure

The probability that two countries adopt a common settlement currency rises non-linearly as the density of shared intermediary partners using that currency increases. A core node defecting triggers a cascade.

Network Closure Equation: $$\mathcal{P}_{ik} = 1 - \prod_{j \in \mathcal{N}} (1 - \alpha e_{ij} e_{jk})$$
Intuition: This probability $\mathcal{P}_{ik}$ shows that as more of your trading partners ($j$) adopt a specific currency, your own pressure to switch rises exponentially. It's the "Peer Pressure" of the global financial system.

2. DeGroot-Nash Belief Dynamics

A country's confidence in the USD is formed through non-Bayesian social learning from its trade partners. We identify a structural tipping point (τ = 0.42) beyond which a coordinated block causes network defection.

Structural Tipping Condition: $$\rho(\mathbf{W}_-) > \frac{\lambda}{1-\lambda}$$
Intuition: When the "centrality" or influence of an opposing currency bloc (like BRICS+) reaches a critical mass (the spectral radius $\rho$), the network hits a point of no return where de-dollarization becomes the dominant strategy.

3. Centrality-Adjusted Gravity

While standard macroeconomic gravity models rely heavily on GDP and distance, we embed GNN-extracted network centrality. Structural centrality dictates reserve currency share exponentially, outpacing static GDP factors.

The Centrality Gravity Term: $$T_{ij,t} \propto \frac{M_i M_j}{D_{ij}} \cdot \exp(\theta \cdot C_i \cdot C_j)$$
Intuition: Traditional models look at size ($M$) and distance ($D$). Our model adds **Centrality** ($C$). This reveals that even if a country's GDP ($M$) is huge, losing network connections causes its currency share to collapse exponentially.

Case Study: Post-Sanctions LPG Realignment

The 2022 Post-Sanctions Energy Shock

The energy shortages and supply chain realignments that followed the 2022 geopolitical shock—particularly concerning Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG)—provide empirical validation of the CGN framework.

The Triad is Broken: Russia forced USD invoicing to zero for energy exports. India and China shifted to alternate currencies, severing the shared USD intermediary loop.
Systemic Network Evolution: Forging non-dollar settlement infrastructure expanded the DeGroot influence, pushing the BRICS+ network to the τ = 0.42 tipping point.
Centrality Eclipse: Bypassing Western clearinghouses caused a sharp decline in the USD's structural network centrality ($C_{i,t}^{\text{USD}}$), driving an exponential decay that eclipses GDP mass effects.
